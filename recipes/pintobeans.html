Pinto Beans

  • Home

Description

Me gustan los frijoles pintos. Nunca los ha cocinado pero si los ha comido.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dry pinto beans
  • 1 tablespoon avocado oil
  • 1/2 white onion, chopped
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons cumin
  • 8 cups water, more as needed
  • 1/2 teaspoon oregano
  • 2 teaspooons fine sea salt, more to taste
  • freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice, more to taste

Steps

  1. First, soak the beans. Place them in a large colander and sift through them to remove any stones or debris. Rinse them well and transfer them to a large bowl. Cover them with 2-3 inches of water and set them aside to soak for at least 8 hours, or overnight.

  2. The next day, cook the aromatics. Sauté half an onion in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat (you could also toss in a jalapeño if you like your beans spicy!). When the onion softens, stir in cumin, the soaked, drained beans, water, oregano, salt, and pepper.

  3. Then, simmer. The cooking time will depend on the freshness of your beans and how you like them cooked. I cook my pinto beans until they're falling apart and the liquid around them has thickened. I start checking after an hour and again every 15 minutes after that.

  4. Finally, season to taste. When the beans are cooked to your liking, season them with a squeeze of lime juice, more salt and pepper, and chili powder to taste.
